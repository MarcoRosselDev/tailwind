Panqueques con manjar

Pancakes are versatile and perfect for Chilean afternoon tea.

Ingredients

  • ¾ (180 ml.) cup of whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons of butter, melted
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of granulated sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon of salt
  • ¾ cup (110 grams) of all-purpose flour
  • 16 tablespoons of filling

Instructions

  1. In a blender or food processor, combine the milk, melted butter, eggs, sugar, and salt. Blend for a few seconds until smooth.
  2. Add the flour and blend until smooth. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  3. Take the mixture out of the refrigerator about 30 minutes before making the pancakes and whisk with a balloon whisk to lighten it a little, 30 seconds.
  4. Heat a non-stick frying pan about 23-25 ​​cm in diameter over medium-high heat until a few drops of water sprinkled on it dance and jump before evaporating. Add 1/4 cup or 60 ml or half a ladle of batter to the pan, swirling the pan so the batter covers the entire bottom. Cook for 45-60 seconds, and when the edges begin to brown and lift slightly, use a spatula to flip the pancake and cook for another 45 seconds. The first one won't turn out perfectly, but it's still edible.
  5. Stack the cooked pancakes on a plate and keep them covered with a clean kitchen towel so they don't get cold.
  6. To serve, spread about 2 tablespoons of dulce de leche or your favorite jam on the pancake, and fold it into quarters or roll it up. Sprinkle with powdered sugar and serve.
  7. Once cooked, the pancakes can be frozen well wrapped in kitchen plastic wrap then in aluminum foil for up to 2 months, thaw on the counter, fill and heat in the oven at 300F or 150C or in the microwave.
